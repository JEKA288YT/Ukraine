Ukraine

Information

Ukraine (Ukrainian: Україна) is a state in Eastern and Central Europe. Population: 40,997,699 permanent residents and 41,167,336 current population. Total area - 603,549 km² (44th place in the world); largest country by area entirely in Europe The capital and largest city is Kyiv. The official language is Ukrainian Ukraine is a unitary state and a mixed republic. The post of President since May 20, 2019 is held by Vladimir Zelensky, Prime Minister since March 4, 2020 - Denis Shmyhal.

It borders Romania and Moldova to the southwest, Hungary, Slovakia, and Poland to the west, Belarus to the north, and Russia to the east and northeast. In the south, it is washed by the Black and Azov seas.

Interesting Facts

The first constitution in the world, which outlined the rights of citizens and the government, was developed and put into effect in 1710 by the Ukrainian hetman Pylyp Orlyk. For comparison, the US constitution was drafted and adopted in 1787.

Zaporizhzhya Sich is a socio-political and military-administrative organization of the Ukrainian Cossacks, which was founded in 1553-1554, today it is considered one of the first democratic organizations in the world.

Ukraine gave up the third largest arsenal of nuclear weapons in the world (after the USA and the Russian Federation). At the time of the declaration of independence, more than 1,000 nuclear warheads and missiles were located on the territory of Ukraine.

On the territory of Ukraine is the cradle of world civilization, older than the Egyptian pyramids and Stonehenge - the Stone Tomb complex in the Zaporizhzhia region.

In 1929, the USSR declared that about 60% of all explored gas reserves were concentrated on the territory of Ukraine, after which the country became one of the largest exporters of blue fuel to Europe for a certain period of time. And today, Ukraine ranks 3rd in terms of the number of shale gas exploration data in Europe, ahead of only Poland and France.

Ukraine has the largest reserve of manganese ore in the world, 2.3 billion tons or about 11% of all deposits on Earth.

1/4 of all chernozem reserves on Earth are concentrated on the territory of Ukraine.

Ukraine has its own Maldives - amazing beaches with white sand and clear blue sea. They are located on the protected island of Dzharylgach, mutilated by Russia, in the Kherson region. Note that local flora and fauna are listed in the Red Book, and mustangs and camels used to live here
